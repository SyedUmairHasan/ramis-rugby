Rugby




Rugby football originated at Rugby School in Rugby, Warwickshire, England, one of many versions of football played at English public schools in the 19th century, including association football. In 1895, Northern rugby clubs split off and formed the Northern Union. Although they initially continued to use the Rugby Union's rules, ultimately a separate game, now known as rugby league, evolved. The first rugby match in North America was played between McGill University and Harvard University. Both American football and Canadian football evolved from rugby football.
